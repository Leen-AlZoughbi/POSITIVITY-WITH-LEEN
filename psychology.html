Positive Psychology

We become what we think about

Those who practice positive psychology attempt psychological interventions that foster positive attitudes toward one's subjective experiences, individual traits, and life events. The goal is to minimize pathological thoughts that may arise in a hopeless mindset, and to, instead, develop sense of optimism toward life.

The positive psychology movement aims to improve individuals and populations with greater social and economic outcomes through a focus on psychological factors that promote personal growth. It is used in a variety of contexts, including educational settings, health care, criminal justice, employment, criminal rehabilitation, and women's studies. Positive psychology also has been used to promote many types of sports, such as swimming, volleyball, and football.The Positive Psychology Network has become an international organization to promote, facilitate, and disseminate research, applications, resources, and support for the psychology of the positive.
